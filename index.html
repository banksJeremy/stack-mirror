//&>/dev/null;x="${0%.*}";[ ! "$x" -ot "$0" ]||(rm -f "$x";cc -o "$x" "$0")&&exec "$x" "$@"

This is my most useful. I put it at the top of any simple single-file C scripts, allowing me to execute them directly. It compiles if necessary and calls the executable, passing on any parameters.
